Dr. Alan Turing

The Father of Computer Science



A Brief History


Dr. Alan Turing was born on the 23rd of June 1912, in London England. A highly intelligent child, with an aptitude for math and science, Dr. Turing played a critical role in the Second World War by leading a group known as Hut 8, a section of the Government Code and Cypher School.

Dr. Turing and his team developed methods to increase the speed at which German cyphers were broken; his work allowed the Allies to win pivotal battles against the Nazis.

Following the war, Dr. Turing worked at the National Physics Laboratory where he worked on the designs of the first stored-program computers.

Of his work, what may be most notable today as we enter the age of Artificial Intelligence, is what is known as the Turing Test. This is a test of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behaviour well enough to be indistinguishable from a human.

Dr. Turing is remembered as a hero of the Second World War, a brilliant man, and for his incredible contributions to the field of Computer Science.
